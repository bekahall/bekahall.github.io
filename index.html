Rebekah Hall

rah11@sfu.ca

I am a MSc student in the Department of Mathematics at Simon Fraser University, working with Dr. Ailene MacPherson. My complete CV can be found here.

My current research seeks to use stochastic simulations and mathematical modelling to answer fundamental eco-evolutionary questions. I am using differential equation models to synthesise the theories of local adaptation and life-history evolution within the context of the seasonal environments. I have also worked on a simulation framework for life-history evolution in microbial range expansion. Previously, I worked with Dr. Daniel Charlebois developing stochastic spatio-temporal simulations of yeast colony growth. My final undergraduate honours project focused on numerical methods for reaction-diffusion models of cell polarisation, supervised by Dr. Jay Newby.
